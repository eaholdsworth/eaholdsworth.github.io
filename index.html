About Me

Elizabeth A. Holdsworth:
Biocultural anthropologist studying
biological mechanisms of developmental plasticity and
intergenerational social inequality

I am a biocultural anthropologist identifying the biological (epigenetic and microbiome) mechanisms and environments that shape infant growth and development, focusing on maternal-infant relationships.

Research

I have conducted research as a principal investigator, co-PI, and investigator on multiple projects relating to biocultural determinants of health, intergenerational influences on fetal and infant development, epigenetic and microbiome mechanisms of developmental plasticity, and the Developmental Origins of Health and Disease (DOHaD). The following are some current and past projects I have participated in:

Immigrant Mothers and Infants in Upstate New York

2017-2021, Co-PI

This project used a combination of biocultural anthropology fieldwork and secondary data analysis to answer questions about how social inequality patterns exposure to stress and how this may be epigenetically transmitted across generations to affect child growth. I used a combination of mixed-methods ethnographic research in rural Upstate New York among immigrant mothers and local service organizations and an analysis of the Avon Longitudinal Study of Parents and Children to answer these questions. You can find out more about this project at the NSF Project page here.
Fieldwork dataset on figshare

Mother-Infant Microbiomes, Behavior, and Ecology Study (MIMBES), PI Dr. Courtney Meehan

2021-Present, Postdoctoral Research Associate and Investigator

This research project explores questions about human milk microbiome, maternal-infant relationships, and infant gut microbiomes. To find out more about this project, check out the Biocultural Anthropology Lab at Washington State University.

Breastfeeding and Cannabis Use, PI Dr. Courtney Meehan

2021-Present, Postdoctoral Research Associate, Co-PI, and PI on Follow-Up Study

This project seeks to describe the pharmacokinetics of cannabis consumption in human milk. I am leading a 3 month follow-up to further evaluate infant development. To find out more about this project, check out the Biocultural Anthropology Lab at Washington State University.

Albany Infant and Mother Study (AIMS), PI Dr. Allison Appleton

2015-2021, Research Assistant and Investigator

This project explored the joint contributions of maternal psychosocial and environmental factors to prenatal development. You can find out more about the study here. See some of our publications from this project about maternal social determinants of fetal growth, maternal adverse childhood experiences and birth outcomes, neighborhood conditions and birth outcomes, and prenatal lead and depression exposure influences on NR3C1 methylation in cord blood.

Disparities in COVID-19 Exposure and Outcomes in Albany, NY

2020-2021, Investigator

This collaboration between local community members and an interdisciplinary team of researchers at the University at Albany explored disparities in COVID-19 along economic and racial lines in Albany NY. You can read about some of our findings here.

Teaching
